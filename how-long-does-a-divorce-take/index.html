How Long Does a Divorce Take in Australia?

From the 12-month separation requirement to the month-and-a-day after the order, the divorce itself runs on a fairly predictable timeline. But property and parenting follow their own clock entirely. Here's how long each part really takes — and how to avoid the delays that catch people out.

Key takeaway: Before you can even apply, you must be separated for at least 12 months. Once filed, a divorce is typically processed within a few months, and the divorce order takes effect one month and one day after it's granted. Property and parenting matters run on separate timelines — quick by agreement through mediation, or one to three years if contested in court.

The short answer

There are really two timelines. The divorce itself: 12 months' separation before applying, then a few months to process, then one month and a day until it's final. The property and parenting side: as little as a few weeks if you mediate, or one to three years if it's fought in court. Most of the "how long does divorce take" frustration comes from conflating the two.

The 12-month separation rule

You can't apply for divorce until you've been separated for at least 12 months and one day, per the Family Law Act 1975. This is the biggest single component of the timeline, and it can't be shortcut. The good news is you don't have to wait for the divorce to sort out property and parenting — those can (and usually should) be resolved during the separation period. See our separation guide →

Processing and hearing times

Once you file, processing typically takes a few months, depending on court workload. Whether you need to attend a hearing depends on your situation — joint applications with no children under 18 often proceed without anyone attending, while sole applications involving children may require a brief hearing. See the full application process →

The one-month-and-a-day rule

After a divorce is granted, it doesn't take effect immediately — it becomes final one month and one day later. Only then are you legally divorced and free to remarry, so factor this into any plans.

When the divorce becomes final

The divorce order takes legal effect one month and one day after it is granted. At that point you'll be able to access the divorce certificate, and you're free to remarry. Remember, too, that this final date starts the 12-month clock to apply for property orders — a deadline people frequently overlook. See property time limits →

Property and parenting timelines

This is where the timeline varies wildly, and where you have the most control. Resolved by agreement through mediation, property and parenting can be settled in a matter of weeks. Fought through the court system, the same issues can take one to three years. The choice of path — far more than the divorce paperwork — determines how long your separation really takes to finalise. How to avoid delays

  1. Use the separation period productively — resolve property and parenting before the divorce is even granted.
  2. Apply jointly where possible — it avoids service and often avoids a hearing.
  3. Get the application right — errors cause rejections and delays.
  4. Mediate the substantive issues — it's the single biggest time-saver.
  5. Watch the 12-month property deadline after the divorce is final.

Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions

How long does a divorce take in Australia?+

You must be separated 12 months before applying, then processing takes a few months, and the divorce becomes final one month and one day after it's granted.

Do I have to wait 12 months to get divorced?+

Yes. You must be separated for at least 12 months and one day before you can apply. However, you can resolve property and parenting during that time.

When is a divorce final in Australia?+

The divorce order takes effect one month and one day after it's granted. Only then are you legally divorced and free to remarry.

Why do property matters take longer than the divorce?+

Because property and parenting run on a separate timeline. By agreement through mediation they take weeks; contested in court they can take one to three years.

How can I make my divorce faster?+

Resolve property and parenting during the separation period, apply jointly where possible, get the application right, and mediate the substantive issues to avoid court delays.
