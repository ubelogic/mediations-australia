Workplace Mediation in Australia: How It Works

Workplace conflict is costly — in productivity, morale and staff turnover. Workplace mediation resolves disputes between colleagues, teams, or staff and management confidentially and constructively, before they escalate to formal proceedings. Here's how it works and when to use it.

In this guide

  • What is workplace mediation?
  • What disputes can it resolve?
  • How the process works
  • The Fair Work Commission's role

Key takeaway: Workplace mediation is a confidential, voluntary process where a neutral mediator helps people in a workplace dispute reach a practical resolution. It resolves conflicts between colleagues, teams, or staff and management — from communication breakdowns to bullying complaints — before they escalate. It's faster, cheaper and less damaging than formal grievance processes or Fair Work claims.

What is workplace mediation?

Workplace mediation applies the same principles as family mediation to disputes at work: a neutral, impartial mediator helps the people involved talk through the issues and reach a practical, agreed resolution. It's confidential and voluntary, and the mediator doesn't take sides or impose an outcome — the participants shape the solution themselves. It can be used proactively, well before a dispute reaches the Fair Work Commission.

What disputes can it resolve?

  • Conflict between colleagues or within a team
  • Disputes between staff and management
  • Communication breakdowns and personality clashes
  • Bullying, harassment or grievance complaints
  • Conflict following organisational change or restructure
  • Disputes about roles, responsibilities or expectations

How the process works

  1. Referral and agreement — the parties agree to participate voluntarily.
  2. Individual meetings — the mediator meets each person to understand their perspective.
  3. The mediation session — the parties work through the issues with the mediator's guidance (jointly or shuttle-style).
  4. Agreement — practical commitments are agreed and documented.
  5. Follow-up — sometimes a check-in confirms the arrangement is working.

Confidentiality makes it work

Because workplace mediation is confidential, people can speak honestly without fear it will be used against them later. That candour is often what finally breaks a long-running workplace deadlock.

The Fair Work Commission's role

The Fair Work Commission offers conciliation and mediation for certain workplace matters, such as unfair dismissal and general protections disputes. Many workplace conflicts, though, are better addressed before they become formal Fair Work matters — private workplace mediation can resolve underlying issues early, avoiding the cost, stress and adversarial nature of a formal claim. The benefits for employers and staff

  • Faster and cheaper than formal grievance processes or litigation
  • Preserves working relationships rather than entrenching division
  • Confidential — protects reputations and morale
  • Reduces turnover and the cost of losing good staff
  • Empowering — participants own the solution, so it tends to stick

When to bring in a mediator

The best time to mediate is early — before positions harden and the conflict spreads through a team. If you're seeing persistent tension, repeated complaints, breakdowns in communication, or a dispute that internal processes haven't resolved, it's time to consider a neutral mediator. Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions

What is workplace mediation?+

A confidential, voluntary process where a neutral mediator helps people in a workplace dispute reach a practical, agreed resolution. The mediator doesn't take sides or impose an outcome.

What workplace disputes can be mediated?+

Conflict between colleagues or teams, disputes between staff and management, communication breakdowns, bullying or grievance complaints, and conflict after organisational change.

How is workplace mediation different from a Fair Work claim?+

Workplace mediation is private, early and constructive, resolving underlying issues before they escalate. A Fair Work claim is a formal, more adversarial process for matters like unfair dismissal.

Is workplace mediation confidential?+

Yes. Confidentiality lets participants speak honestly without fear it will be used against them, which is often what resolves a long-running workplace dispute.

When should an employer use workplace mediation?+

Early — at the first signs of persistent tension, repeated complaints or communication breakdowns, before the conflict hardens or spreads or becomes a formal claim.
